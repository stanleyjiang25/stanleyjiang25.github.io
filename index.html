Interests

Apart from my academic interests in Computer Science and Mathematics, I enjoy drawing, playing the clarinet/piano, and exercising. Although I preferred pencil sketches when I first started drawing in 2017, I have recently started learning how to create digital drawings on my iPad after filling out my sketchbook. Unlike my continued passion for drawing, my relationship with creating music has been more tumultuous. Despite my introduction to the piano at the age of 5, I was never a piano prodigy. Amongst my stagnating improvement and shrinking passion, I decided to stop taking pianos lessons to focus on the clarinet in 7th grade. After a series of heartwrenching audition mishaps and results, the same frustrations returned. However, I was unable to abandon the clarinet ... and music ... for long. Surprisingly, when I forced myself to remove music from my life, I realized that I loved every aspect of it -- listening to it, studying music theory, and creating it. When I picked up the clarinet again, I played only for myself. I also started to relearn the piano after not playing it for over 4 years! In truth, both instruments helped me remain sane during the pandemic. I spents hours learning the Stamitz Clarinet Concerto No. 3/Saint-saens Clarinet Sonata and Lizst's Liebestraum No. 3/Chopin's Fantaisie Impromptu on the Piano. When I'm not drawing or making music, I enjoy exercising and letting my mind wander.
